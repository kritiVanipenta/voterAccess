Voting in Elections 101

Federal Level

  • Presidential Elections
    • held every 4 years
    • choose California’s electors for the Electoral College
  • U.S. Senate Elections
    • each Senator serves a 6-year term
    • California has 2 Senate seats, each voted separately
  • U.S. House of Representatives Elections
    • held every 2 years for all 52 congressional districts
    • determines California’s representation in the House of Representatives

State Level

  • Governor and Executive Offices – every 4 years (includes Lt. Governor, Attorney General, Secretary of State, etc.)
  • State Legislature
    • State Assembly: 2-year terms, all seats
    • State Senate: 4-year terms, half seats every 2 years
  • Ballot Measures (Propositions) – vote on laws, constitutional amendments, or bonds
  • Primary Elections – “Top two” system: all candidates on one ballot, top two move to general election

County Level

  • elect leaders like Sheriff, District Attorney, Board of Supervisors, etc.
  • typically held during statewide elections (June primary and November general)
  • affects local services such as law enforcement, taxes, and education administration

City & Municipal Elections

  • elect mayors, city council members, city attorneys, and other local officials
  • some align with statewide elections, others in odd-numbered years
  • may include ballot measures for taxes, housing, or city services

School District & Special District Elections

  • school boards oversee K–12 and community college districts, approve funding or bonds
  • special districts manage water, fire, transit, and utilities
  • held alongside statewide elections or in special local elections
